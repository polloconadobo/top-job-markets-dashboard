Data Sources & Methodology

All data sourced from free, authoritative US government and industry databases — no API key required

Live Data — Fetched Fresh on Every Refresh

BLS CES Series CES5000000001 Current Employment Statistics — Information sector jobs (thousands), seasonally adjusted. The most current monthly read on tech-adjacent employment. Monthly
BLS JOLTS Series JTS51000000000000JOL Job Openings & Labor Turnover Survey — open IT positions in Information sector. Tracks actual unfilled demand in real time. Monthly
BLS CES Series CES5000000003 Average hourly earnings, Information sector — used to track wage growth trends in tech-adjacent roles. Monthly

Annual / Static Data — Current as of March 2026

BLS OEWS May 2024 Occupational Employment & Wage Statistics — median annual wages for IT Support Specialist (SOC 15-1232), Systems Administrator (SOC 15-1244), and national median. Released November 2024, most current available. Annual
CompTIA Cyberstates 2026 Total US tech workforce count (10.3M+), state-level tech employment rankings, skills demand analysis. Industry's most comprehensive annual tech labor report. Annual
NCWIT Women in Tech 2025 Women's representation in computing workforce (27%), historical gender trends since 1960s, role-specific breakdowns for infrastructure vs software. Annual
EEOC EEO-1 Aggregate Data 2024 Race and ethnicity breakdown of US tech workforce. Hispanic/Latino representation (8.5%), trend data from 1995–2024. Annual
Stanford WFH Research Lab 2025 Remote & hybrid work rates in tech sector (34% as of Q1 2026). Most rigorous longitudinal tracking of post-COVID remote work trends. Quarterly
Glassdoor & Levels.fyi Q4 2025 Cloud / Azure Engineer salary estimates ($142K median) — BLS does not have a specific SOC code for cloud engineers, so industry salary platforms are used. Quarterly
LinkedIn Workforce Insights 2026 Top in-demand skills in US IT job postings as of March 2026 — chart updates based on your uploaded résumé. Monthly
NBER / EEOC Age Discrimination Data Research on age-related hiring bias in tech, documenting slowdown beginning at age 45+. Used for age distribution analysis and career timing recommendations. Annual
Why This Approach (Free Public APIs — No Key Required)

This dashboard uses the BLS Public Data API v1 — a completely free, unrestricted US government API that requires no registration, no API key, and no account. It powers all live data refreshes and works on any device, in any browser, including mobile.

Demographic data (gender, race/ethnicity, age) updates annually from NCWIT and EEOC — not in real time — so those figures reflect the most recent annual releases and are accurate for 2025–2026.

Salary data for standard IT roles comes from BLS OEWS (released annually each November). Cloud engineer salaries use Glassdoor and Levels.fyi because BLS does not yet have a dedicated SOC code for cloud engineering roles.

A cover letter should do one thing the résumé cannot: tell a story. The résumé lists what you did. The cover letter explains why it matters to this specific employer. Save the supporting context for the interview, where you can read the room.
